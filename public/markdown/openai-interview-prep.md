## One-sentence answer

OpenAI's interview process for IC engineering and research roles is a 4-to-6-stage gauntlet covering system design at AI-scale, ML fundamentals, an alignment / safety conversation, a code-and-debug session in your strongest language, and a values-fit / "research taste" round — AimVantage (formerly Vantage AI) generates a prep pack against the specific OpenAI JD you're applying for with company intel, fit score, mock interview questions, and a tailored cover letter.

## Typical OpenAI interview pattern (2026)

1. **Recruiter screen (30 min)** — motivation for OpenAI specifically, salary expectations, timeline.
2. **Hiring-manager call (45 min)** — depth on your last 1–2 projects, why this role, why now.
3. **Technical screen (60 min)** — depending on track:
   - Engineering: code-and-debug in your strongest language. Often a small but mean problem with edge cases.
   - Research: ML fundamentals + a paper-walkthrough of one of your published or referenced works.
4. **System design / scale (60 min)** — pragmatic, applied. Expect: "How would you build a low-latency inference proxy in front of GPT-X?", "How would you cache embeddings at scale?", "What's your rate-limit strategy?"
5. **Alignment / safety conversation (45 min)** — open-ended. They want to see how you think about AI safety, not memorized definitions. Common: "What's your view on capability evals?" / "When would you publish a model vs delay?"
6. **Values-fit (45 min)** — research taste, intellectual honesty about what you don't know, prior examples of changing your mind under evidence.

## Most-asked OpenAI interview questions (likely-templates)

- **System design:** Build a key-value store optimized for embedding lookup. Optimize a streaming response pipeline. Cache LLM outputs without leaking PII.
- **ML fundamentals:** Why does RLHF work? What goes wrong in supervised-only fine-tuning? Walk through attention mechanism end-to-end.
- **Alignment:** Where do you draw the line on model release? How would you red-team a new capability? When is a benchmark misleading?
- **Coding:** Implement a rate limiter (token bucket variant). Detect cycles in a directed graph. Streaming median.
- **Behavioral:** Tell me about a time you changed your mind on a major technical decision. When did you ship something you knew was imperfect, and why? What's a paper you initially disagreed with that later changed how you work?
