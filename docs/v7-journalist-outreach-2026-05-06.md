1. **Indie / build-in-public stories** — angle: "UK solo founder shipped X in 60 days, paying himself nothing" — Sifted, UKTN, IndieHackers
2. **Tooling angles** — angle: "Free, client-side ATS scanner for job seekers" — UKTN, TechRound, dev-focused newsletters
3. **Anti-incumbent / category-pricing angles** — angle: "Pay-per-use AI vs $50/mo subscriptions in the resume-tool category" — Sifted (markets/competition), Lenny's Newsletter (PM angle)

**Single best angle for first wave:** the *free in-product ATS preview alongside paid tool* — concrete, specific, novel. Not "AI-powered". Specific capability + measurable benefit (5 vendor parsers, no upload, instant).

## Anti-patterns (do not do)

1. **Do not send mass-personalised templates** — journalists detect them in seconds.
2. **Do not lead with "AI-powered"** — the AI narrative is crowded, generic adjectives are dead.
3. **Do not pitch TechCrunch / WSJ / FT on launch news** — too small to be newsworthy. Wait until first paying-customer milestone, ARR threshold, or partnership.
4. **Do not embargo, do not lock copy, do not create artificial urgency** — indie pitches with embargos read as cosplay.
5. **Do not copy other indie founders' pitch decks** — every journalist has seen them.
6. **Do not include attachments on first email** — many filters bin them.
