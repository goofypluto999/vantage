**Tweet 2:**
```
1/ The first number is anchoring, not the offer.

Recruiters anchor low because expecting a counter is the norm. If you accept the first number, you've left the recruiter's "headroom" budget on the table — they're trained to expect a 10-15% counter and they have flex for it.
```

**Tweet 3:**
```
2/ Negotiate on three axes simultaneously: base, signing, RSU.

Recruiters have least flex on base, most on signing.

Asking only on base = high-friction
Asking on all three = signals you understand comp structure = lands at higher total even if base barely moves
```

**Tweet 4:**
```
3/ Never negotiate on a phone call in real time.

When they verbally improve the offer:
"Thank you. I'd like to think about it overnight and confirm tomorrow."

Always 24 hours. Always.

Decisions made on the call, accepting verbal improvements live, leaves money on the table.
```

## Posting strategy

- **Mega-threads:** Sundays + Wednesdays. 2 per week max.
- **Single tweets:** 1 per day weekdays, 0 on weekends.
- **Engage:** comment on 5-10 tweets in adjacent niches per day. Higher leverage than original posts when your follower count is small.
- **Quote-tweet** other people's relevant posts (laid-off announcements, hiring posts) with a useful free tool link as the QT.
- **Pin** Mega-thread 1 (the State of 2026 one) for at least 4 weeks unless something more viral happens.

- Don't paste >5 links in one tweet
- Don't post >10 tweets in one hour
- Don't follow >50 people/day from a low-follower account
- Don't quote-tweet your own threads more than once per week
